About Me


Hello all - I'm Lane Barton, a CURRENT_AGE year old programmer from Portland, Oregon currently enrolled in the computer science graduate program at Portland State university. This site should serve as an introduction to myself, some programming projects I have worked on, and additional contact information

Programming Background

My path to being a computer science has not been a linear one. I was a mathematics and physics double major as an undergraduate at Whitman College, where for my first three years on campus there were only two programming course offered - a intro to Python course and a data structures course. But as fate would have it, my senior year the school established a computer science department and began to offer an expanded selection of CS opportunities. I spent a large chunk of my senior year sampling these offerings and fell in love with the problem-solving, critical-thinking, and able to create that is inherent in programming and computer science as a whole

Since my time in Walla Walla, I've worked towards a master's degree in computer science at Portland State University. I've enjoyed learning about a wide range of disciplines under the umbrella of CS, with a particular level of focus on courses in the software engineering, security, and database tracks offered through the grad program. During this time, I've also opportunity to participate in the PCEP internship program, which connects students with local software companies for 6 month part time-rotations. All of these activities has given me an appreciation for the range of technologies and programming languages that can be leveraged to create awesome applications and pieces of technologies. Personal Interests

While programming is my professional passion, there are a number of other interests and hobbies the influence my work. My background in mathematics stems from an interest in data modeling and analytics which translates well into my focus on database management. I have had a litany of ties to writing and public speaking, ranging from Speech & Debate plus an educational competition known as Constitution team in high school and extending through my undergraduate years where I was a writer and news editor for the school paper. This skillset has led to an increased interest in effective code documentation and presentation work for technologies that is clear and understandable for people with different levels of computer science knowledge.

But most of all, I have been a sports fanatic for my entire life. I was shooting basketballs from my crib and attending football games at Autzen Stadium since before I was two years old. I learned to read by pouring over the box scores of the Portland Trail Blazers and Seattle Mariners. I've played soccer recreationally for 20 years and love cheering on the Timbers and Thorns in their professional setup in SW Portland. This deep love for sports and the Pacific Northwest which houses my hometown teams is etched into my DNA like the 0s and 1s of a computer program and I enjoy combining these personal pleasures whenever I can.
